Prominent Dance and Music forms of Assam

Most Prominent Dance form of Assam

Bihu Dance

Bihu dance is one of the most colorful and vibrant folk dances of Assam, traditionally performed during the Bihu festival, which celebrates the Assamese New Year and the arrival of spring.
Here are some key points about Bihu dance:
Origins: Bihu dance has its roots in ancient agricultural rituals and traditions of Assam, dating back centuries. It was originally performed by farmers and villagers to celebrate the harvest season and invoke blessings for a prosperous year ahead.
Style: Bihu dance is characterized by its energetic movements, rhythmic footwork, and graceful gestures. The dance is performed in groups, with both men and women wearing traditional Assamese attire and dancing in sync to the beat of the music.
Music: Bihu dance is accompanied by traditional Assamese music, featuring instruments such as the dhol (drum), pepa (buffalo hornpipe), taal (cymbals), and gogona (jew's harp). The music is lively and upbeat, reflecting the festive spirit of the Bihu festival.
Themes: Bihu dance often portrays themes of love, romance, and the beauty of nature, with dancers mimicking the movements of birds, animals, and agricultural activities such as planting and harvesting.

Overall, Bihu dance is cherished for its joyous celebration of Assamese culture, its vibrant costumes and music, and its role in bringing communities together during the Bihu festival.


Most Prominent Music form of Assam

Borgeet

Borgeet is a traditional devotional music form of Assam, composed by the medieval saint and reformer Srimanta Sankardeva. It is an integral part of the Assamese Vaishnavite culture and is sung in praise of Lord Krishna and other deities.
Here are some key points about Borgeet:
Origins: Borgeet originated in the 15th century as part of the cultural and religious movement initiated by Srimanta Sankardeva, who composed numerous devotional songs and hymns in Assamese and Brajavali languages.
Composition: Borgeets are composed in various ragas (melodic scales) and talas (rhythmic patterns), with lyrics inspired by the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita and other Hindu scriptures. The songs are characterized by their simplicity, melody, and profound spiritual themes.
Performance: Borgeets are typically sung in congregational settings, such as prayer halls and monasteries, with singers accompanied by traditional musical instruments such as the khol (drum), taal (cymbals), and manjira (metallic hand cymbals). The music is soulful and meditative, creating a serene atmosphere for worship and contemplation.
Legacy: Borgeet has played a significant role in preserving and promoting Assamese culture and spirituality, serving as a medium of religious expression and devotion for generations of Assamese people. It continues to be performed and revered as a timeless treasure of Assam's musical heritage.

Overall, Borgeet is celebrated for its spiritual depth, musical richness, and cultural significance, and it remains an enduring symbol of Assam's religious and artistic traditions.
